# Key Insights & Recommendations

## Overview
This analysis examines customer churn behavior for a subscription-based service, focusing on churn trends, customer tenure, subscription plans, usage behavior, and payment issues. The goal is to identify key drivers of churn and provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention.

### 1. Churn Trends by Signup Cohort
- Customers who signed up in more recent months tend to churn faster than earlier cohorts.
- Average tenure varies significantly across signup cohorts, indicating differences in onboarding effectiveness over time.
- Early churn appears to occur within the first few months after signup.
  
**Implication:** Early customer experience and onboarding play a critical role in long-term retention.

### 2. Churn by Subscription Plan
- Premium plan shows a higher churn rate compared to Basic, despite higher pricing.
- Users on the Basic plan tend to have shorter average tenure.
- This suggests pricing alone does not drive retention; early user engagement may be more critical.
  
**Implication:** Pricing alone does not guarantee retention; perceived value and product experience matter more.

### 3. Usage Behavior and Churn
- Users with low weekly usage show significantly higher churn rates.
- High-usage users demonstrate stronger retention and longer tenure.
- This suggests that early engagement is a key driver of customer retention.
  
**Implication:** User engagement is a strong predictor of churn risk.
  
### 4. Payment Issues and Churn
- Customers with multiple payment failures show significantly higher churn rates.
- Users with zero payment failures demonstrate the strongest retention.
- Payment friction contributes directly to customer attrition.
  
**Implication:** Improving billing reliability can directly reduce customer churn.
